Traditional healthcare systems in India include various indigenous practices such as Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani, Homeopathy, Naturopathy, and Yoga. These systems have a long-standing history and continue to be widely practiced across the country, often alongside modern allopathic medicine. There are numerous facilities dedicated to traditional healthcare, with reports indicating the existence of 1,369 hospitals that cater to traditional medicine, with a combined bed capacity of over 53,000. Traditional practitioners play a significant role in providing healthcare, especially in rural and underserved areas. These systems emphasize holistic approaches to health and wellness, integrating natural remedies and lifestyle practices. Despite the growth of modern healthcare infrastructure, traditional medicine remains an integral part of India's healthcare landscape, reflecting the country's rich cultural heritage.
